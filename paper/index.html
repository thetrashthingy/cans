Why should paper be recycled?

Recycling paper is crucial for environmental sustainability. Paper waste accumulates in landfills, contributing to deforestation and greenhouse gas emissions. Recycling paper reduces the need to cut down trees and conserves natural resources. By choosing to recycle paper, we can minimize our environmental impact and create a more sustainable future.

Examples of Recyclable Paper:

  • Cardboard, Office Paper, Newspapers and Magazines
  • Mail that is no longer needed and Junk Mail.. etc..

How to Recycle Paper:

  1. Remove Staples and Binder Clips: If possible, remove these items from paper.
  2. Flatten Cardboard Boxes: Flatten boxes to save space.
  3. Place in Recycling Bin: Look for the designated recycling bin for paper.

Convincing Yourself to Recycle:

  • Think of the Forests: Help protect forests by reducing the demand for paper from trees.
  • Conserve Resources: Save energy and water by recycling paper, as it requires less energy and water to produce recycled paper.
  • Reduce Landfill Waste: Minimize the amount of paper waste that ends up in landfills.

By consistently recycling paper, you can contribute to a healthier planet for future generations. Let's make recycling a part of our daily lives and work together to reduce paper waste.
